The problem

One alert. Hours of manual detective work.

When a crypto transaction alert fires, an AML analyst can't just look at that one transaction. They have to reconstruct context — and it's slow, repetitive, and doesn't scale as volume explodes.

Painpoint 01 · Context

"Have we seen this pattern before?"

The analyst digs back through prior transactions and past cases to judge whether this is a real threat or a repeat false positive — manually, every time.

→ Clario: the RAG memory instantly recalls the most similar resolved cases and their outcomes, so every decision is grounded in precedent.

Painpoint 02 · Regulation

"What do the rules say, in this country?"

Crypto regulation differs by jurisdiction and keeps changing (Travel Rule, thresholds, sanctions regimes). Analysts re-check policy by hand.

→ Clario: policy is retrieved and applied by the agents today; a dedicated Regulation Agent (roadmap) will check crypto law by jurisdiction automatically.

Why crypto first? Because the ledger is public and structured — a clean, verifiable substrate for an AI agent to investigate end-to-end, the perfect wedge to prove agentic compliance.

The same agents run on retail banking rails — payroll, e-wallet, remittance, card — where the real pain is volume: thousands of alerts a day. Clario triages by segment and SLA, fast-tracking the routine so analysts focus only on what's risky.

RAG Orchestrator

Memory is the moat.

Every resolved case becomes institutional memory. When a new alert arrives, the Orchestrator retrieves the most similar prior cases — with their dispositions and analyst notes — so the agents don't investigate from zero. The more Clario runs, the sharper it gets.

Deterministic policy gates always sit on top: a confirmed sanctions or mixer hit forces escalation regardless of what the model thinks. Explainable by design — every decision cites the signals and precedent behind it.

Why we're building Clario

A builder's bet on agentic compliance.

I'm not an ex-compliance PhD. I'm a builder who kept seeing the same thing: AML analysts drowning in alerts, doing hours of manual detective work per case — pulling transaction history, chasing counterparties, re-reading regulation.

We chose crypto as our first market on purpose. The blockchain is public and structured — the one place an AI agent can investigate with real, verifiable data instead of guesswork. It's the ideal proving ground for agentic compliance.

Clario is that proof: a team of AI agents that does the analyst's legwork end-to-end, keeps a human in control of the final call, and leaves an audit trail a regulator can trust. From here, the same agents extend across every transaction rail — starting with retail banking.
